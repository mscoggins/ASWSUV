    Learn about this app's features:

    • Each button above the map toggles a different set of markers.
    • All of the building markers are linked to information about that building, including an image gallery. Some other markers have info windows that pop up after being tapped.
    • The 'Events' page holds all of the events for the upcoming months. Additionally, the 'Events' icon on the home page tells you how many events are scheduled for today.
    • You can take notes on the 'Notes' page and save them for later.
    • The 'Directory' page holds information about all of the staff and services on campus, including Student Affairs and ASWSUV and OSI.

    About This App

    The ASWSUV app was originally was created by Mike Scoggins, Zak Steltz, and Melody Jensen in Fall 2011 for a class project as 'WSU Vancouver Tour', an app only available in the iphone app store. Over the following summer, Mike teamed up with ASWSUV Director of Communications Derick Lock to revamp the app and publish it as a student utility with the full support of ASWSUV for the purpose of making it easier for students to navigate the campus and get involved.

    Give us Feedback

    If you have any suggestions for changes to this app or for the ASWSUV in general, we encourage you to give us feedback by emailing us at aswsuv.dpr@vancouver.wsu.edu.

    Report a problem

    Likewise, if you notice a problem with the app or some part of the app doesn't work for you, we encourage you to alert us of the problem by emailing us at aswsuv.dc@vancouver.wsu.edu.

    Department Offices Student Affairs ASWSUV/OSI

    The Bookstore

    Hours: Monday-Thursday from 9:00am to 6:00pm and Friday from 9:00am to 5:00pm

    This is the campus bookstore, which is located on the bottom floor of the SRC. Here you can find textbooks for your classes, school supplies, WSUV gear and other class materials. The Bookie also buys back textbooks at the end of each semester, even if you didn't buy them from the campus bookstore.

    The Cafeteria

    Dengerink Administration Building (VDEN)
    Hours are Mon-Thur 8:00am-6:00pm and Fri 8:00am-4:00pm

    The campus cafeteria makes great sandwiches, burgers, chicken strips and much more. It is also a great place to study and meet together with friends. The cafeteria also has a microwave if you need to heat up your lunch, coffee or cup of noodles.

    Commons Cafe

    Firstenburg Student Commons
    Hours vary

    This is a small cafe located in the corner of the student commons. If you are hungry while studying, playing ping-pon or just hanging out, this convenient cafe is here to satiate.

    Recreation

    FIrstenburg student commons on the side closest to VSCI

    The Recreation Office is the place where students can get resources for outdoor activities. Here students can sign up for trips, sports games, and rent equipment. We also have a basketball court, soccer fields and a disc golf field. There are also many trails for you and your friends to explore and observe wildlife from. If thats not enough, check out the events page for upcoming rec events. Have fun.

    Fitness Center

    Basement floor of Libray, between VLIB and VMMC

    The Fitness Center is an excellent place to work on your cardio or tone your body. This 2,340 sq. ft. room includes treadmills, stationary bikes, free weights and more to help keep you in shape. In addition to equipment, the Fitness Center also offers group classes, personal trainers and other ways to stay healthy and fit. To learn more, stop on in or check out their web page onlin.e

    Computer Labs

    Located in VMMC, VCLS, VLIB, and VCCW.

    There are several computer labs around campus that are open to all students and faculty which are fully equipped with Macs and/or PCs which include many different programs to help with projects and hw. If you have trouble you can always speak to the supervisor. There are also printers in these labs.

    Parking Services

    VPP

    You can buy your parking passes and pay off parking tickets in the Parking Services Office. The lost and found is also located in this office.

    Library

    VLIB

    The WSUV library may not have many books, but we have access to numerous online databases and the library staff is very knowledgeable about navigating the resources. They can help you find a good start on your research. There are also several study spaces and computers for homework.

    Campus Security

    VDEN, on the side facing the courtyard between VDEN and VCLS.
    Emergency Towers located around campus.

    The campus police offer many services to students free of charge such as escorts to your car, accessing your car if you are locked out, and jump starting your car if it dies. The Police on campus are commissioned policeman of the state and work hard to prevent crime. For emergencies, call 911 on any campus phone. To reach the campus police office, call (360) 546-9001.

    Back Attractions

    Rock Sculptures


    "Opening the Secret," by artist Li Xiu Qin, is a rock sculpture created from volcanic basalt native to the Pacific Northwest region. Large stones are arranged to resemble huge books and split open to reveal secret messages in American and Chinese Braille portrayed through concave openings and bronze rods. The sculpture was formally dedicated on July 14, 1999 and is located behind the campus library.

    Golden Statue


    The statue is dedicated to Lori Irving. Created by a local group of artists called Women Who Weld, this sculpture features bronze pillars that depict themes in Lori Irving's life. These themes include Teaching/Mentoring; Feminism; Positive Body Image; Community; and Laughter and Dance. The pillars are meant to signify simplicity and strength - standing tall on solid ground which embodies Lori's life and the fulfillment she reached in her 38 years.

    Firstenburg Family Fountain


    The Firstenburg Family Fountain was a gift from Ed and Mary Firstenburg. It is a year-round fountain which consists of four distinct water features. It offers seating areas where students, faculty, staff and visitors can relax and enjoy views of Mount St. Helens to the north and Mt. Hood to the east.
